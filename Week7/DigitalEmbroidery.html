Week 7 - Digital Embroidery

Embroidering and Sewing a Drawstring Bag

For my design, I had a theme in mind of a fairy wing/tattoo esc situation. You can see some of the sketches on the right but the final design is the dark outlined image on the left. Once I was happy with my drawing, I scanned it with my phone and airdropped it to my mac. I imported that image into Adobe Illustrator and worked from there.

You can see that the image, when first imported, is super pixelated and rough. I played around with the image trace and went with the black and white logo option in order to expand it into a smooth vector.

Now that the image is expanded, you can see that it is still crooked in many areas. The pixels are no longer a problem but the design doesn't look crisp. I went ahead and took a couple hours (an exhausting process) to select each spot to make more even lines in my design.

My fabric of choice for the outside was black so I wanted my design to pop. I wanted to go for a sort of "edgy" feel and chose red. For the text I chose white, because I thought it would match best with the fabric lining and design.

I thought that for the text, I wanted it to be cursive so that it matched the aesthetic of the design. I tried some straight fonts but they did not look as good. I changed the color to brown just for the sake of the text being a different color. Premier + and our machine allow you to change colors around as long as they're seperated.

I exported the design as a pdf so that I could access it into Premier +. I wanted the bag to match a sort of aesthetic I had in mind. That being, edgy, cool, minimalistic, etc. I though the colors and design helped embody that although it still being a logo for an alleged business.

Here is Premier+. Now that my design is saved as a pdf, I express the design into the hoop and it will show as an embroidered design.

When I imported my design, Premier+ actually showed the text to be brown but I went ahead and edited that to be white. Just for the sake of knowing what it would look like. It doesn't technically matter though since I can physcially swap out threads when embroidering.

Here is the embroidery machine. You can see it is set up for embroidery right now, but it has the ability to sew as well. I have the plastic Q foot attached and all of my thread threaded. I then attached the hoop and was all set up. I sent the design from Premier+ to the machine. There is a small icon that looks like a folder with a heart, on the embroidery machine, and that is where we can access sent files. I dragged my design in, centered it, and clicked go.

(Imported Embroidered Design)

Here's an action shot!

After my design was finished, I peeled off the sticky backing that I applied before embroidering. This backing ensured a secure process when the machine mrboidered.

This is the final image. The letters aren't as clear as I'd hoped they'd be but with a smaller design, it was slightly expected.

Now it was time to sew the actual bag. I started by cutting out the fabric pieces I would need. (I was extremely sick so forgive me for the bad pictures and lack of them) The tutorial we used asked for two outer pieces (12.5"x12.5"), two inner lining pieces (12.5"x12"), and two pieces for the drawstring (12.5"x2"). In this image im creating 1/4" hems on the drawstring pieces.

I used my own sewing machine, for the sake of not booking up the machine when others might need it. I used the 11 stitch which is a simple straight stitch.

In this image, I have attached the outer piece to the lining, with the drawstring piece folded in between. I also added an extra stitch across the top so that it would lay flat.

Here I am sewing the linings together and the outer piece as well. They are being sewn together so that you don't see them seams. I left a 3" opening in order to flip it right side out.

This is the small opening I left.

I pinned the seam so that it would lay flat.

I decided that I wanted to crochet the cords for the drawstring feature!

Here is the final bag with the lining!
