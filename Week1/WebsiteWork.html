Week 1 - Making a Website!

~A Learning Process~

Considering I joined this class a week after it started, I was very worried to get this website done in two days to catch up... but if you are able to read this I guess it's a good sign!? After continously bothering the TA's in the groupchat, (thank you Whitney and Tuna) I was able to piece something together. I have done a little html and CSS before this so I watched all of our suggested videos as a refresher which really helped. What you're looking at below is my start to this coding process all from the terminal of my computer.

After getting the terminal, github, brackets, and VSC set up I was able to copy a template from w3schools to give me a base and I continued from there. For my test run, it started with a simple "Hello World" which you see above.

At this point, I copy and pasted the w3templates code and pasted it into VSC to then edit into a website specific to me. The first thing I started with was changing the navbar and image which can be seen in the photo below.

Here is the result of my first push! I would be lying if I said I got this on the first try, it took some trial and error for me to learn that images and files need to be in specific folders so that they link to the index.html. Thankfully I was able to stop by the lab and solve my first error with a TA!! I uploaded a photo I took and had to play around with the sizing options so it could fit a wide screen and then moved on to my "About Me" section.

This is the coding for my "About Me" section. I was having issues with the layout of my image and text because they were all centered and in my opinion looked a bit awkward. I messaged the TA's to help me figure this out and we inserted, "class="w3-col m6", this helped my page have specific borders and columns so that everything alligned well and looked a bit less messy.

My next area of error was a bit stressful and caused me to fall behind. My navbar was not reactive! I created folders to fall under each assignment in my exisiting folder named "aniakopian.github.io" but the links were not reactive and caused me to have nonexistent pages. I scoured google and w3schools but couldn/t fully understand the issue. I then learned that the issue simply lied with having an extra space and missing a slash. Code am I right. The picture to the left shows what was popping up for hours when clicking the dropdown option of "Making a Website"

My navbar was now reactive and took you to the page you're reading now! To get to this point of my assignment a copied the original index.html coding and pasted it into a new html named WebsiteWork.html. I then got rid of some of the layout sections that were not necessary since this page requires a fairly simple layout. I made sure to use the coding from earlier that alligns my text and images and it was smooth sailing from there! For the ongoing future I will be doing the same with each assignment and linking them under the corresponding folders for the week.

For getting this done in a little under 72 hours I think this page came out alright. Definitely a lot of room for growth and learning but I am happy with the new skill I gained. The tutorials and guideline were extremely helpful in creating this page a long with the TA's that answered every question I had. I hope this page can slowly progress into it's full potential throughout the semester.
