Final Project Week!

Digitally Fabricated Bag

Welcome to my final project! The start of this process involved creating a few designs/ideas as to what we wanted to create. I have been really interested in the concept of combining fabric and 3D printing. I have two designs here involving that process, one being a shirt and one a bag. My other design explores the concept of 3D design and its possible effect on dimension to a room through lighting. I was thinking of how the light could shine through depending on the design and structure of the lamp.

I had my consultation with Whitney and we went through my designs one by one. We thought that the bag could be an interesting idea, especially to explore 3D printed textiles. I will be needing to use the sewing machine, digital mebroidery machine, and the 3D printer. The bag I am interested to play around with to see how functional it can continue to be with the added features. I want to maybe try putting a 3D printed pocket inside of the bag and see how it works. Ultimately, I also want the 3D printed features to bring a cool design in that can be surrounded by embroidery.

For my first prototype I will be testing my embroidery. The quote I chose is "Comme une Fleur", "Like a Flower". I chose this A.) because my 3D printed features are going to be small flower prints and B.) because the phrase is mainly used in France in an ironic way for someone who comes unprepared. I think it sounds as if it could be a nice brand name but also has a funny quirk since your bag can help you come prepared to places!

In Adobe Illustrator, I first chose a nice clean font that is crisp enough for a "brand" name. I then created the small flower design that will tie into the rest of the bag. I created the flower with the pen tool.

Then, to create the center of the flower, I used the brush tool. I readjusted the size to fit with the phrase.

I imported my design as a pdf into Premier+ and made some small adjustments beforen sending it to the embroidery machine.

My fabric hasn't come in yet, but luckily I had some extra fabric of my own that is a similar material to what I'll be using. I tested out the design and it worked pretty well. I think I will definitely use a different thread and maybe play around with the font because Premier+ somewhat fused the letters together.

Here is the final quote! For a first run, I'm not mad about it. Like I said, I will definitely continue to play around with it some more.

We are now entering 3D printing territory! I am going to show a few process photos involved in creating my design. With my small amount of knowledge on Fusion 360, I had to create my flower shape. I played around a bit and saw the best way was to creat a line sketch first. I used the line tool that creates curved lines, and drew out a flower. Now, the flower was a bit wonky at first, so I had to move the points around a bit to adjust.

I then extruded the flower, not too much, because this is going to act as a sort of "textile" on my bag.

I used the hole feature to create the centers and made sure to set the distance to go all the way through.

Here's the final design. I added a curved line as a guide when I moved the flowers around. It acts as the exterior of my bag and allowed me to envision nthe design as if it was already there.

I picked my filament color to test this design out and started the print! I let the machine create a first layer, paused it, then set the fabric up on the bed.

I definitely have some things to fix for my final piece. To start, when sewing the bag, I used two layers of fabric on both sides to give some structure but it is not enough. I am going to use this material as the lining, and I found a thicker material that looks identical which I can use instead. For the 3D print, I would honestly like to add some more, I think I will add more flowers to the bag and change the filament to black. This way everything looks cohesive and there is obvious texture. When sewing, I need to play around with measurements a bit more. I want to maybe add an inch to the circumference, and make the straps a bit longer. Finally, I also want to add some features to the bag. 1.) I want to add a surrounding base to give more room withing the bag. 2.) I want to include a pocket of some sort. 3.) I think some sort of closing feature could be beneficial as well.
