Week 4 - SLA Printing

Printing a Ring

SLA Printing or, stereolithography, is a type of 3D printing that has a similar objective to FDM printing in regards to the models it creates. FDM printing uses a process of extruding plastic filaments, whereas SLA uses a compound resin which is liquid. The resin is pulled up by the buildplate and hardens through UV light. Once the print is done, you need to cure and wash your model. For this, we use the Form 1 and 2. You first remove the pieces from the build plate, and then place them in the wash. Once the model is clean, you will cure it for a final time to make sure the resin is sturdy.

For my ring, I chose the tutorial for a fidget spinner ring by Urza. I was having some issues with this video since it moved at such a fast pace. I just changed the playback speed to a slower pace and paused whenever necessary. I had trouble keeping track of where the lines had to be placed and in what order, the first round of creating the ring, this caused me problems. I then went back, rewatched, and took things step by step. I had a much better result and had all the lines connect at the correct measurements. I enjoyed that Urza was very detailed with his steps and showed us the whole process as well as explanations for certain steps in Fusion.

One of the first new things I learned in Fusion 360 was linework. We normally only created shapes with either the rectangle or circle tool. Now we learned to use symmetrical lines that connect to create specific shapes.

One of the next things I learned was creating a revolve feature. After finishing the linework, if you click finish sketch, the lines connect to create the interior shape of the fidget feature. Once this is done, if you click on revolve, you will then need to select your two faces, and then the axis you need your ring to revolve around. This then creates your ring shape.

Another feature I learned was section analysis, this allows for you to make sure all you interior features look correct and will be functioning properly.

The next new feature I learned was something we have previously done. We have filleted edges on our previous projects, but I have never filleted the interior with multiple corners. I held shift and selected multiple edges to create soft edges that will allow the spinner to move smoothly.

Urza's tutorial also taught us on how to add text and then engrave it into the ring. I had to enter the circumference of my ring to make sure the text would fit all the way around, since my name is really short this was not an issue. The text initially pertrudes out of the ring, I changed this so that it was engraved.
