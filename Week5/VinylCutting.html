Week 5 - Vinyl Cutting

Designing a T-Shirt

Here is our vinyl cutter! The machine that helped produce the shirt right up there!^^ Now, the question is whether or not vinyl cutting could be considered a form of digital fabrication? In my eyes, yes. This view could be argued, but I believe that since digital fabrication is identified as a form of data transoformation to create models and designs it does tie in with vinyl cutting. The process of vinyl cutting involves first, deigning a graphic, then importing it into a software ("Sure Cuts A Lot") which has the ability to render and send your design to the cutter to then cut out the design. This process does involve understanding programs such as Adobe Illustrator, Sure Cuts A Lot, and ultimately knowing how to use the vinyl cutting machine.

For my design, I wanted to create something based off of one of my favorite albums. Kanye West's "Graduation". The main graphic on the cover is a little cartoon bear so I wanted to throw that in along with a quote from the song "Everything I Am". I went and searched google for black and white graphics of the bear for my design.

The next step after downloading the bear graphic was to get some more fonts that would pair nicely with my design. I found these free fonts that can be dowloaded to Adobe Illustrator and went with those. After this, I was ready to start my design.

You can see that my graphic is very pixelated and rough around the edges so we need to fix that. This can be solved with an image trace. This process transforms our image from a raster to a vector. A raster image is made up of tiny pixels and is typically better suited for more realistic or intricate photos. By doing our image trace, we modify this image into a vector which is a graphic rendered by illustrator typically used most for simple designs (logos, signage, etc).

After doing the image trace and then expanding it, we need to remove the excess white background so the bear is simply isolated. To do this we select the background with the white seleciton tool and then press delete.

Here you can see the image can now freely move and intertwine with the rest of my design. Behind the bear graphic, you can see the text I included was a song lyric, "Everything I'm Not Made Me Everything I Am". I played with some of the new fonts I downloaded and went with mainly the 3D version with the exception of two words.

With my finished design, I saved it on Adobe Illustrator and sent it to our computer that is connected to the vinyl cutter. From here I needed to measure my vinyl, t-shirt, and the length I wanted my design to go. I added these dimensions into Sure Cuts A Lot and made sure to flip the design!! This way it will not press on backwards.

After sending the design to the printer, it began slicing the design which you can see a bit of the bear in this image!

Once the design was printed, we had to start the process of weaving. Weaving is essentially just removing the excess pieces of vinyl that involve our negative space. Here you can see I already removed the parts of the bear that are going to be white once they are on the shirt.

Here is the final design after I finished weaving! It can now be layed onto the shirt before hitting the heat press.

This is our heat press, in this photo it is heating up, but once it is set we press down the bar until it locks. We only hold it down for 10 seconds and then lift. After this, we are able to peel off the plastic and it leaves us with our finished shirt!
