Week 8 - Integrate

Creating an Acrylic Sign

Here is the start of my design process for this weeks assignment! For my design, I went with the Kanye Graduation Bear from my vinyl assignment, only because this project had so many steps. I copy and pasted that design and used the acrylic file we were given.

I used that file and worked it into my design. I had it outline my design and deleted the line above the indents so that glowforge doesn't cut it out. Once I was happy with the design, I saved it as an SVG and imported it to Glowforge.

This is the imported design. I used the engrave setting for the design, and the cut setting for the exterior lign. I also made sure it all measured out okay to fit my future box.

I insterted my acrylic sheet into the machine and once it focused, I dragged my design on top to make sure it all aligned.

Here is the machine in action!

This is the sign once it was done being cutout, all I had to do was peel off the paper.

Here it is all peeled! I actually had no idea what color I picked since it was mainly covered, I thought it was going to be orange but I was pleasantly surprised!

Our wooden inserts and electronics were pre-made for us so I did not need to worry about that. The next step was to design my case for the electronics in Fusion360. I measured the diamter of my wooden inserts and got to work!

I made sure to be super specific with the measurements since I need the wooden pieces to fit right in and be stable. I left a small slot in the back in order for the power cord to have access to the lights.

I offset the rectangle to give it some thickness and then extruded it in order to reach an appropraite height to be a stand.

Here is the final design I came up with to hold my wooden pieces and acrylic sign. I saved it as an STL and sent it over to Professor Dunaway to print since the lab was occupied during my appointment.

This is a picture of our electronics. I originally had a training where we made our own but we then had new ones made for us along with our wood files which you are looking at here. Unfortunately my piece was not working. The piece where our power cord inserts immediately popped off. I asked our TA Whitney for help and when we put the piece back on, it still would not work when connected. We came to the conclusion that the HE Tiny must have overheated, thus the board not functioning.

Once I came to pick up my 3D printed case, everything fit along with the power cord piece. The only issues was that the board wouldn't lay too well so I had to make the slot slightly bigger. I got a saw and measured how much I needed to remove. Once that was done everything fit smoothly.

Here are the pieces that you can see through the back.

This is just a bird's eye view of the case without the top piece that holds the sign.

The final product! Unfortunately I could not get the lights to work in time for this projects submission, but I hope that I can fix the electronics and show it lit up!
