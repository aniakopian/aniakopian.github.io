Week 6 - Laser Cutting

Making a Box with Glowforge

For my box, I went with the heartbox design. I then made sure to add a lid as well as change the thickness of the material to 3.12mm. I clicked generate and the site provided me with my design and I saved it in the form of an svg.

Here is what our program generated for this design. The image is a bit too large to fit on the page but you get the jist of things. After this, I save the dsign as an SVG to import it into Adobe Illustrator and continue with the design.

Now that the design is in Adobe illustrator I added my initials in the font I like. You later will notice I switched it to my name instead of initials. I then rotated the font and created an outline of it.

After doing the image trace and then expanding it, we need to remove the excess white background so the bear is simply isolated. To do this we select the background with the white seleciton tool and then press delete.

Once the design was finished, I saved it as an SVG again and imported it into Glowforge. Glowforge is our laser cutter that also uses this software with it. I imported the file and then made adjustments to the settings. I made sure to "cut" the areas that I wanted to be completely cut through. For my name, I went with a "score", so that my name would be outlined but not completely cut through.

This is the Glowforge Laser Cutter! Here I turned the cutter on and autofocused the machine.

This is the air filter attached to Glowforge so that we do not start any fires while printing!

Once the filter and machine were on and ready, I was set to start printing. I clicked the start button on the machine and my print took about 20 minutes.

Here you can see the cuts being made from the start.

Some more action shots :D

After the 20 minutes were up, I waited for the laser cutter to cool down before opening. Once cooled, I grabbed all my pieces and got to qork on putting them together.

Most pieces fit together on their own but for a few points I had to do some glueing. I put wood glue on the areas where the heart curves and put some clamps on overnight. This did the trick and in the morning I came back to my finished product.
